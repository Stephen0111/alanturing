Alan Mathison Turing

The father of modern computer science

Below is a brief outline of Alan Turing's life

  • 1912 - Alan Turing was born on the 23rd of June, in Maida Vale, London.
  • 1918 - Alan Turing joins St Michael's day school in Hastings.
  • 1922 - Alan Turing is sent to Hazelhurst Preparatory School.
  • 1926 - Alan Turing joins Sherborne School. Alan Turing cycled 97km from his home on his first day of school.
  • 1928 - Alan Turing develops a keen interest in Maths and Science. Alan encountered Albert Einstein's works. Turing meets Christopher Morcom
  • 1930 - Turing's close friend Christopher Morcom dies from tuberculosis. Turing is devastated and renounces his religious faith.
  • 1931 - Alan Turing joins the prestigious King's College in Cambridge
  • 1934 - Alan Turing is awarded a first-class honours degree in Mathematics
  • 1935 - Alan Turing is elected fellow of King's College for his dissertation Central Limit Theorem. Turing attended Max Newman's advanced course in Mathematics.
  • 1936 - Alan Turing joins the Institute of Advanced Studies at Princeton where he started to study Cryptography and Mathematics
  • 1938 - Alan Turing obtains his PhD from Princeton University.
  • 1939 - Alan Turing joins the Government Codes and Ciphers School and arrives at Bletchley Park
  • 1942 - Alan Turing and his colleagues break the German Naval Enigma system.
  • 1943 - Alan Turing is asked to work as a top level intelligence link with USA.
  • 1946 - Alan Turing and his group pioneer modern computer programming , writing a library of sophisticated programs for the unbuilt ACE.
  • 1947 - Alan Turing returns to Cambridge for a sabbatical year.
  • 1948 - Alan Turing is appointed Reader in the Mathematics department at the University of Manchester. World's first stored-program electronic computer comes to life.
  • 1951 - Alan Turing is elected Fellow of the Royal Society FRS.
  • 1953 - Alan Turing publishes his classic paper on computer chess.
  • 1954 - Alan Turing dies of cyanide poisining in Wilmslow, Cheshire.
